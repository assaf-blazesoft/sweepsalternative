## What is CardCrush?

CardCrush is a single-currency online game platform wrapped in a role-playing-game (RPG) progression system. Players advance a character, unlock content, and play card-based games, without the dual Gold Coin / Sweeps Coin economy that defined the now-banned sweepstakes casino model.

It is one of the first platforms to position itself explicitly as a **legal alternative** for players in states like California and New York, rather than simply pulling out of those markets.

## How the legal model works

The sweepstakes bans that swept through 13 states in 2025 and 2026 targeted a specific mechanic: a **dual-currency** system where a "free" coin and a redeemable "sweeps" coin run in parallel, functionally recreating real-money casino play.

CardCrush uses a **single currency**. There is no parallel redeemable coin running alongside a play-money coin. That structural difference is what its operators argue places it outside the scope of statutes like California's AB 831, which are written specifically around the dual-currency design.

This is a newer legal posture than, say, [parimutuel gaming](/guides/parimutuel-explained), which has decades of regulatory history. That relative novelty is the main reason we assign CardCrush a **medium** risk rating rather than low.

## California availability

CardCrush is available to California players as of our last verification date. Because AB 831 is written around the dual-currency mechanic, a genuinely single-currency product is structured to fall outside it. That said, California's Attorney General has been aggressive in this space, and a single-currency label alone is not a guarantee, the substance of how the currency works matters.

## New York availability

CardCrush also serves New York players. New York's combined SB 5935 / AB 6745 ban likewise focuses on dual-currency sweepstakes. As with California, availability today does not guarantee availability tomorrow; this is a fast-moving area.

## Other states

CardCrush's single-currency model is generally portable to other banned states, but availability varies and the company rolls out state by state. Check the platform directly for your specific state before assuming access.

## How to sign up

Registration follows the standard pattern: create an account, verify your email and age, and confirm you are physically located in a supported state. Single-currency platforms typically require identity verification before any cash-out, so expect a KYC step.

## Redemption experience

Because CardCrush runs a single currency, the redemption experience differs from a classic sweepstakes cash-out. Review the platform's current terms for exactly how winnings convert and what verification is required, this is the area most likely to change as the model matures.

## Our verdict

CardCrush is a credible, genuinely-available option for California and New York players who want something close to the old experience. We rate it medium risk: the single-currency model is sound in principle but younger and less tested than the parimutuel alternatives. Treat it as promising rather than proven, and keep an eye on its legal status.
