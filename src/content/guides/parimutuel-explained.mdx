## The core idea: a shared pool

The word *parimutuel* comes from the French for "mutual betting." Here's the entire concept in one sentence: **everyone's wagers go into a shared pool, and the winners split that pool.**

Compare that to a casino. When you play a slot machine or a hand of blackjack, you're betting **against the house**. The casino wins when you lose, and loses when you win. The house has a direct financial stake in the outcome of your game.

Parimutuel works differently. The operator doesn't bet against you. Instead:

1. All players' wagers on an event go into a pool.
2. The operator takes a fixed percentage off the top, this is called the **"takeout."**
3. Whatever remains is divided among the players who wagered correctly.

The operator makes the same money whether you personally win or lose, because they're paid from the takeout, not from your losses. This structural difference, house-versus-player betting versus player-versus-pool betting, is at the heart of why parimutuel gaming is regulated so differently from casino gambling.

## Where it comes from: horse racing

If parimutuel betting sounds like horse racing, that's because horse racing is exactly where it was perfected. For over a century, the standard way to bet on a horse race has been parimutuel: you bet into a pool, the odds shift based on how everyone else bets, and the pool pays out after the race.

This long history matters enormously for legality. Parimutuel wagering on horse racing is woven into both state law and **federal law**, most notably the **Interstate Horseracing Act**, which provides a framework for legal, cross-state wagering. **Advance-deposit wagering (ADW)**, funding an account in advance and wagering from it, including online, grew directly out of this framework.

## Why the sweepstakes bans don't touch it

This is the key point for anyone arriving from a banned sweepstakes casino. The 2025–2026 bans, California's AB 831, New York's SB 5935, and the rest, were written to target one specific thing: the **dual-currency sweepstakes structure** with its Gold Coins and redeemable Sweeps Coins.

Parimutuel gaming has none of that. There's no dual currency, no "free" promotional sweeps coin, no sweepstakes framing at all. It's a different activity under a different body of law. The new bans simply don't reach it, which is why parimutuel platforms continue operating in states like California even after the sweepstakes model was outlawed there.
