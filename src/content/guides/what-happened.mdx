If you're reading this, there's a good chance you opened your usual sweepstakes casino one day and found you could no longer play. You're not alone, and you didn't do anything wrong. The law changed underneath you. This guide explains exactly what happened, why, and what it means for you as a player.

## The short version

Between mid-2025 and mid-2026, **13 states effectively banned dual-currency sweepstakes casinos.** Nine did it through brand-new laws written specifically to outlaw the model. Four did it by enforcing gambling laws that were already on the books. Several more states are moving in the same direction right now.

The result: tens of millions of Americans who legally played casino-style games online suddenly couldn't anymore, at least not on the platforms they were used to.

## What a "dual-currency sweepstakes casino" actually was

To understand the ban, you have to understand the model it targeted.

Sweepstakes casinos ran on **two currencies**:

1. A **"Gold Coin" play-money currency** you could buy or earn, used for casino-style games with no cash value.
2. A **"Sweeps Coin" promotional currency** you generally received as a bonus (often when buying Gold Coins), which *could* be redeemed for cash prizes.

The legal theory was that because you never *bought* the redeemable Sweeps Coins directly, you got them "free" alongside a Gold Coin purchase, the activity was a promotional sweepstakes, like a fast-food-chain contest, rather than gambling. No license required.

For years, that theory held up well enough for the model to grow into a multi-billion-dollar industry serving players in nearly every state.

## Why regulators decided it was gambling

The dual-currency structure worked legally only as long as regulators accepted the framing. By 2025, most stopped accepting it.

The core objection was simple: **in practice, the model functioned as real-money online casino gambling.** Players were spending money, playing slot- and table-style games, and redeeming winnings for cash. The "free" framing of the Sweeps Coins looked, to a growing number of state attorneys general and legislators, like a technicality wrapped around what was substantively unlicensed gambling.

Three concerns drove the crackdown:

- **Consumer protection.** Unlicensed operators weren't subject to the same responsible-gambling, age-verification, and dispute-resolution requirements as licensed ones.
- **Tax and licensing revenue.** States with regulated online gaming markets watched untaxed, unlicensed competitors capture players they'd licensed others to serve.
- **Tribal and existing-operator pressure.** In many states, established gaming interests, tribal operators, licensed casinos, regulated sportsbooks, pushed hard against what they saw as unlicensed competition.

## The timeline

The bans didn't happen all at once. They cascaded:

- **New Jersey (A5447, Aug 2025)**, one of the earliest statutory bans, from a state with a mature licensed online gaming market.
- **Connecticut (SB 1235, Jun 2025)**, a tightly controlled gaming state closing the gap.
- **Montana (SB 555, Oct 2025)**, a conservative-gaming state making the prohibition explicit.
- **New York (SB 5935 / AB 6745, Dec 2025)**, the second-largest market, a major domino.
- **California (AB 831, Jan 1, 2026)**, the largest market of all, and the most-watched ban.
- **Tennessee (SB 2136) and Louisiana (HB 883), May 2026**, the most recent statutory bans.
- **Indiana (HB 1052, Jul 1, 2026) and Maine (LD 2007, Jul 14, 2026)**, passed and dated, taking effect in mid-2026.

Alongside the statutory bans, four states reached the same result through **enforcement of existing law**: Washington (under its felony internet-gambling statute), Michigan (via the Michigan Gaming Control Board), Idaho (under its constitutional ban on casino simulations), and Nevada (where operators largely self-excluded under the state's enforcement powers).

And the watch list keeps growing: **Washington DC, Minnesota, Illinois, and Iowa** are all in various stages of banning or restricting the model as of this writing.
