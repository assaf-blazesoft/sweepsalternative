## Why sweepstakes casinos got banned in New York

New York's **SB 5935 / AB 6745** package took effect in **December 2025**, ending the dual-currency sweepstakes casino model. As in California, lawmakers targeted the Gold Coin / Sweeps Coin mechanic that functioned as unlicensed online casino play. Compliant operators withdrew from the state.

New York matters enormously here: it's the second-largest banned market by population, and "sweepstakes casino alternatives New York" is one of the highest-volume searches in this entire space. For the national picture, see [what happened to sweepstakes casinos](/guides/what-happened).

## What still works in New York

New York is **more restrictive than California**, and that shows up in the options:

- **Single-currency platforms** ([CardCrush](/review/cardcrush), [Albumza](/review/albumza)) are available and structured to fall outside the dual-currency ban.
- **Parimutuel gaming** has a partial footprint, [Horseplay](/review/horseplay) offers some New York access.
- Several California-legal options are **not** available in New York.

## What to look for in an alternative

In New York especially, be skeptical of any platform that can't clearly explain its legal basis, the state's strictness means grey-area operators carry more exposure. Single-currency and partial-parimutuel options are your realistic field today. Confirm current New York availability directly with each platform before depositing, since access here is more likely to shift than in more permissive states.
