## Why sweepstakes casinos got banned in New Jersey

New Jersey moved early. **A5447** took effect in **August 2025**, well ahead of the wave that hit California and New York later in the cycle. That's notable because New Jersey already has one of the most developed *licensed* online gambling markets in the country, so the state had both the regulatory machinery and the motivation to shut down unlicensed dual-currency sweepstakes quickly.

The practical effect for players: operators exited, and the legal alternatives that remain are the same model-based options we track elsewhere. See [what happened to sweepstakes casinos](/guides/what-happened) for the wider story.

## What to look for in an alternative

Because New Jersey has a robust licensed-gaming framework, be especially clear-eyed about which alternatives are genuinely operating under a different legal model versus quietly skirting the rules. Favor established [parimutuel](/guides/parimutuel-explained) and DFS models, and verify current New Jersey availability with each platform before depositing.
