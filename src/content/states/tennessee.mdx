## Why sweepstakes casinos got banned in Tennessee

Tennessee's **SB 2136** took effect in **May 2026**, one of the most recent bans, landing right alongside Louisiana. Tennessee has no commercial casinos and runs a deliberately narrow legal-gaming framework (its regulated online sports betting market is a notable exception). Dual-currency sweepstakes casinos didn't fit that framework, and SB 2136 closed the door.

Because this ban is so recent, operator exits may still be settling. See [what happened to sweepstakes casinos](/guides/what-happened).

## What to look for in an alternative

With a fresh ban, double-check that any platform you consider is actually compliant and available in Tennessee today rather than relying on slightly older information. Established legal models are the safest starting point, and verification matters more than usual right after a ban lands.
