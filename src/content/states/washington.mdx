## Why sweepstakes casinos are restricted in Washington

Washington is different from the statute-based bans. It didn't need a new law, it relies on **RCW 9.46.240**, a long-standing statute that classifies internet gambling as a **Class C felony**. That's one of the harshest online-gambling postures in the United States, and it predates the sweepstakes era entirely.

Because of that, dual-currency sweepstakes operators largely avoided or exited Washington well before the 2025–2026 legislative wave. We classify Washington as **restricted** (enforcement-based) rather than freshly banned. See [what happened to sweepstakes casinos](/guides/what-happened).

## What to look for in an alternative

Washington's strictness means you should be especially careful. The state's broad internet-gambling prohibition shapes what can legally operate there, so verify any platform's Washington status directly and prefer models with the clearest legal grounding. When in doubt in Washington, assume more caution rather than less.
