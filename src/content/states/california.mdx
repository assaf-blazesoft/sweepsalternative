## Why sweepstakes casinos got banned in California

California's **AB 831** took effect on **January 1, 2026**, explicitly outlawing the dual-currency sweepstakes casino model. The law was written around the specific mechanic these platforms used: a "free" Gold Coin currency running in parallel with a redeemable Sweeps Coin, which regulators concluded recreated real-money online casino play without a gambling license.

California's Attorney General had signaled this was coming for over a year, and most major operators exited the state on or before the effective date. The result: millions of California players who suddenly had nowhere to play, and a lot of confusion about what, if anything, is still legal.

For the full national timeline, see our guide on [what happened to sweepstakes casinos](/guides/what-happened).

## What still works in California

California is actually one of the **better-positioned banned states**. Because AB 831 targets the dual-currency mechanic specifically, platforms built on other legal models continue to operate:

- **Parimutuel gaming** ([Horseplay](/review/horseplay)) rests on decades of established wagering law, the lowest-risk option.
- **Single-currency platforms** ([CardCrush](/review/cardcrush), [Albumza](/review/albumza)) avoid the banned dual-currency design, at slightly higher risk because the model is newer.

## What to look for in an alternative

The single most important question is: *what legal model does this platform use, and why does that keep it legal in California?* A credible platform will answer clearly. Favor the established models ([parimutuel](/guides/parimutuel-explained)) and treat newer single-currency products with appropriate caution. Always confirm the platform still serves California before depositing, this space changes monthly.
